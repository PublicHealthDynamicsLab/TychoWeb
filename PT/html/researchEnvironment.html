Research Environment

Project Tycho benefits from a supportive research environment in Pittsburgh, PA, United States, including a world-class University, Biomedical, and Corporate System.

University of Pittsburgh

Public Health Dynamics Lab

The Public Health Dynamics Laboratory (PHDL) is an interdisciplinary center at the Graduate School of Public Health focused on the development of computational methods to improve the theory and the practice of global health. The PHDL includes over 40 faculty and staff, including epidemiologists, biostatisticians, behavioral scientists, public health policy experts and computational scientists to produce the next generation of analysis tools for the exploration of critical public health problems. Project Tycho is part of the PHDL, currently directed by Dr. Mark Roberts.

Project Tycho aims to advance scientific discovery and innovation by providing open access to infectious disease data from countries around the world at a high spatial-temporal resolution. Project Tycho re-represents existing data from (inter)national health agencies and research studies into a user-friendly format that can be accessed manually or programmatically.

Vaccine Modeling Initiative

Project Tycho is also part of the Vaccine Modeling Initiative (VMI) is funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and directed by Dr. Donald Burke, Dean of the University of Pittsburgh Graduate School of Public Health. The objective of the VMI is to improve decision-making in the selection of new vaccine products and epidemic control policies. This is done by the development of computational models and simulations of epidemic infectious diseases of global importance.
